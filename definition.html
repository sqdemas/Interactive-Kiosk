What is the Paradox?

To make extremely accurate and specific predictions, recommender algorithms must analyze large quantites of personal data. The Personalization Privacy Paradox refers to the conflict between a user's desire for personalized recommendations and their concern over the use of their personal data required to achieve such accurate personalization.


Understanding the paradox is important to allow individuals to make informed choices about their technology use as well as to facilitate a future of recommender systems which prioritize the actual desires of users.
